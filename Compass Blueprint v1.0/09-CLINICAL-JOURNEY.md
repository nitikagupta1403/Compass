# Clinical Journey

> *"The Clinical Journey is the continuous story of a patient's health, organized as a sequence of meaningful clinical events connected by evidence, decisions, and outcomes."*

---

# Purpose

The Clinical Journey is the central organizing concept of Compass.

Rather than viewing healthcare as a collection of isolated reports, prescriptions, laboratory results, and diagnoses, Compass views care as a continuous journey.

Every clinical interaction contributes to this journey.

Everything else in Compass exists to describe, explain, or support it.

---

# Why Clinical Journey?

Traditional medical records are often organized by document.

Compass is organized by understanding.

Instead of asking,

> "Where is the MRI report?"

Compass asks,

> "What happened to the patient?"

The Clinical Journey answers that question.

---

# What is a Clinical Journey?

A Clinical Journey is the chronological representation of a patient's care.

It preserves:

- What happened
- When it happened
- Why it happened
- Who participated
- What evidence exists
- What decisions were made
- What outcomes followed

A journey transforms isolated information into clinical understanding.

# Clinical Event

The Clinical Event is the fundamental building block of the Clinical Journey.

Examples include:

- Consultation
- Seizure Episode
- Medication Change
- Laboratory Investigation
- Imaging Study
- Procedure
- Hospital Admission
- Surgery
- Diagnosis
- Follow-up Visit

Each event represents something meaningful that happened.

---

# Every Clinical Event Answers Seven Questions

Every Clinical Event should answer:

### 1. What happened?

The event itself.

---

### 2. When did it happen?

Time and chronology.

---

### 3. Why did it happen?

Clinical context.

---

### 4. Who participated?

Care Team members.

---

### 5. What evidence supports it?

Reports, laboratory results, imaging, notes, observations, photographs, videos.

---

### 6. What decision was made?

Diagnosis.

Treatment.

Observation.

Referral.

Medication change.

---

### 7. What happened next?

Outcome.

Follow-up.

Recovery.

Complication.

Progression.

# Time

Chronology is fundamental.

The Clinical Journey preserves the order of events.

Understanding often depends on timing.

Examples:

Medication started before improvement.

Symptoms appeared after surgery.

Laboratory values changed after treatment.

Time gives meaning to clinical information.

---

# Evidence

Evidence supports understanding.

Evidence does not replace the Clinical Journey.

Reports are evidence.

Laboratory results are evidence.

Imaging studies are evidence.

Clinical notes are evidence.

Evidence explains events.

Events explain the patient's story.

---

# Outcomes

Every important decision should lead to an observable outcome whenever possible.

Examples:

Improved

Stable

Resolved

Progressed

Complicated

Ongoing

Outcomes connect decisions to their consequences.

---

# AI and the Clinical Journey

Artificial Intelligence should reason over the Clinical Journey—not isolated documents.

Instead of summarizing PDFs independently,

AI should understand:

- chronological progression
- relationships
- evidence
- treatment response
- clinical reasoning

The Clinical Journey provides the structure required for trustworthy clinical reasoning.

---

# Design Implications

Every feature in Compass should strengthen the Clinical Journey.

Questions to ask:

Does this help explain what happened?

Does it preserve chronology?

Does it strengthen context?

Does it improve understanding?

If not, reconsider the design.

# Founder Notes

## Why does this document exist?

To define the central abstraction of Compass and establish the patient's clinical journey as the primary organizing principle of the system.

## What future decisions should it guide?

Clinical workflows, user experience, AI reasoning, architecture, data organization, and feature design.

## What must never change?

Compass should always organize medical knowledge around the patient's clinical journey rather than around isolated medical documents.
