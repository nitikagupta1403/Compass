# Clinical Intelligence

> *"The purpose of intelligence in Compass is not to replace clinical judgment, but to amplify human understanding through trustworthy reasoning."*

---

# Purpose

Compass is designed to preserve clinical knowledge.

Clinical Intelligence exists to help people understand that knowledge.

Artificial Intelligence is one implementation of Clinical Intelligence, but not its definition.

The role of intelligence in Compass is to organize, explain, summarize, discover relationships, and assist decision-making while always preserving evidence and traceability.

---

# Philosophy

Compass does not ask,

> "How can AI answer medical questions?"

Compass asks,

> "How can intelligence help people understand a patient's clinical journey?"

The difference is fundamental.

Knowledge comes first.

Reasoning comes second.

Generation comes last.

---

# Intelligence Pyramid

```
Clinical Wisdom
        ▲
Clinical Reasoning
        ▲
Clinical Understanding
        ▲
Clinical Knowledge
        ▲
Clinical Evidence
```

Every level depends on the level beneath it.

Without evidence there is no trustworthy knowledge.

Without knowledge there can be no reliable reasoning.

---

# The Role of Intelligence

Clinical Intelligence should:

- Summarize complex medical histories.
- Organize fragmented information.
- Connect related clinical events.
- Identify temporal patterns.
- Explain clinical reasoning.
- Assist communication.
- Surface relevant evidence.

It should never replace clinicians or caregivers.

---

# What AI Must Never Do

Clinical Intelligence must never:

- Invent evidence.
- Hide uncertainty.
- Modify clinical history.
- Replace clinical judgment.
- Present assumptions as facts.
- Remove traceability.

Trust is more important than automation.

```

↓
```

AI participates in the reasoning process.

Humans remain responsible for decisions.

---

# Explainability

Every intelligent output should answer:

What evidence was used?

What events influenced this conclusion?

What assumptions were made?

How confident is the conclusion?

Can a human verify it?

Understanding is impossible without explanation.

---

# Levels of Intelligence

## Level 1 — Organization

Collect and organize information.

Examples

Timeline construction

Document categorization

Medication history

---

## Level 2 — Understanding

Explain information.

Examples

Summaries

Relationship discovery

Chronological reasoning

Trend detection

---

## Level 3 — Assistance

Support human reasoning.

Examples

Referral summaries

Case overviews

Differential diagnosis support

Treatment history

---

## Level 4 — Prediction

Estimate possible future outcomes.

Examples

Risk assessment

Disease progression

Medication adherence

These outputs should always communicate uncertainty.

---

# Human-Centered Intelligence

Compass is designed around collaboration.

Caregivers contribute observations.

Clinicians contribute expertise.

AI contributes organization and reasoning.

The combination produces better understanding than any participant alone.
